How Bikable Is Your City Council District?

The state of bicycle infrastructure varies widely across New York City. This simple index is designed to help you understand how well your neighborhood - and your elected officials are doing - to help protect cyclists and promote low-carbon travel.

The index takes into account only traditional and protected bikeways on city streets, comparing the mileage of these elements with the total road network in the Council District. Protected bikeways are weighted more heavily than traditional lanes in making this calculation. The City also installs shared streets or "sharrows" in some areas, but these features don't provide substantial protection from motor vehicles.

In the future, more information might be considered to improve this index and develop actionable planning tools for neighborhoods. Additional data may include high-crash intersections corridors, existing and planned street treatments such as daylighting at intersections, and traffic counts with an eye towards high truck traffic areas.
